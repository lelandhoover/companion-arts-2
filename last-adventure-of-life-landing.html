Book Description

Maria Hoaglund's The Last Adventure of Life: Inspiring Approaches to Living and Dying is an essential healing tool for anyone exploring end-of-life issues, moving through transition, grieving or interested in developing new perspectives on spirituality, life and death.

This wonderful book has been called "a treasury of hope and healing" that reframes end-of-life as transformation - a new adventure in life. Hoaglund, a hospice counselor and chaplain, leads us on this adventure with inspirational stories, poetry, scripture, prayers and guided meditations that provide comfort and explore the inter-connectedness of all things. In addition, the last chapter of The Last Adventure of Life provides helpful information on unique spiritual healing and relaxation resources.

Author Bio

Maria Hoaglund

Maria "Dancing Heart" Hoaglund is an acclaimed international author, energy healer, UCC minister, and end-of-life coach.

She grew up in Japan, the daughter of Lutheran missionaries. As a result, she is bilingual and bi-cultural, and has utilized this wonderful combination of language and cultural skills throughout her life.

After finishing high school at Canadian Academy in Kobe, Japan, Maria moved to the United States for college. She graduated from Yale University with a B.A., and subsequently received her Masters of Divinity from Chicago Theological Seminary.

In 1985 she began parish ministry at Church of the Crossroads in Honolulu, Hawaii, where she was ordained in the United Church of Christ. She continued in parish ministry for ten years, serving several UCC churches in Hawaii and in the Great Northwest.

The focus of her ministry changed in 1995 when she began working as a Bereavement Coordinator for a Puget Sound hospice. She added spiritual counseling to her repertoire that same year, and continued with this work until the international release of her first book, The Last Adventure of Life.

Her books have received international acclaim and have been widely appreciated by baby boomers, caregivers, care recipients, and holistic practitioners who desire to deepen their spiritual life by examining and releasing their fears around death and transition.
